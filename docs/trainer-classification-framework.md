# Trainer Classification Framework

**Squarerootz — Internal Document**

---

## Purpose

This document provides a standardized framework for classifying trainers in our pool into three tiers. The classification determines:
- Teaching scope and responsibilities
- Compensation bands
- Website presentation and positioning
- Quality expectations and evaluation criteria

Use this framework when onboarding new trainers or re-evaluating existing ones.

---

## Tier Overview

| Tier | Title | Experience | Commitment | Primary Role |
|------|-------|------------|------------|--------------|
| Tier 1 | Industry Practitioner | 3+ years | Ad-hoc | Guest sessions, masterclasses |
| Tier 2 | Subject Matter Expert | 5+ years | Part-time | Course delivery, mentorship |
| Tier 3 | Lead Instructor | 7+ years | Regular | Course ownership, curriculum design |

---

## Tier 1: Industry Practitioner

### Profile
Working professionals currently employed in AI/ML roles who bring real-world, current industry experience to our students. They are practitioners first, trainers second.

### Minimum Requirements

| Criteria | Requirement | How to Verify |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Current employment | Active role in AI/ML at recognized company | LinkedIn, offer letter, or company email verification |
| Experience | 3+ years hands-on in their domain | Resume review, LinkedIn history |
| Production work | Has shipped at least 1 production system | Portfolio review, GitHub, or verbal walkthrough |
| Recency | Active work in teaching domain within last 12 months | Discussion during screening call |
| Technical depth | Can answer intermediate-advanced questions in domain | Technical screening (15-20 min) |

### Preferred (Not Required)

- Active GitHub with relevant contributions
- Technical blog or LinkedIn content
- Conference talks or meetup presentations
- Open-source project contributions

### Teaching Scope

**Allowed:**
- Masterclasses (60-90 min deep dives)
- AMA / Q&A sessions
- Case study presentations
- Career guidance sessions
- Capstone project reviews (with guidance)

**Not Allowed:**
- Full course delivery
- Curriculum design decisions
- Student assessment/grading
- Representing Squarerootz at external events

### Availability Expectation
- Ad-hoc basis, minimum 2 weeks scheduling notice
- No minimum hours commitment
- Can decline sessions based on availability

### Website Persona Elements
```
Photo: Professional headshot
Name: Full name
Title: Current Job Title
Company: Current Employer (with permission)
Tagline: "Currently building [X] at [Company]"
Bio: 2-3 sentences about current work and expertise
Domains: List of teaching domains (e.g., "NLP, RAG Systems, LLMs")
```

### Compensation Band
- Per-session basis
- Refer to compensation policy document

## Tier 2: Subject Matter Expert

### Profile
Deep specialists with extensive experience in specific AI/ML domains. They have the expertise to deliver full courses and mentor students through complex topics. May be industry practitioners, researchers, consultants, or founders.

### Minimum Requirements

| Criteria | Requirement | How to Verify |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Experience | 5+ years in specific AI/ML domain | Resume, LinkedIn, reference check |
| Depth | Expert-level knowledge in at least one course domain | Technical interview (30-45 min) |
| Teaching experience | Prior training delivery (corporate, bootcamp, university, or online) | Past training feedback, certificates, or references |
| Communication | Can explain complex concepts clearly | Demo session evaluation |
| Production exposure | Multiple production projects in teaching domain | Portfolio review, case study discussion |

### Preferred (Not Required)

- Published papers or articles in domain
- Conference speaker experience
- Open-source maintainer or significant contributor
- Recognized certifications (cloud, vendor-specific)
- Prior curriculum design involvement

### Teaching Scope

**Allowed:**
- Full course delivery (all modules)
- Student mentorship and doubt resolution
- Capstone project guidance and evaluation
- Assessment design (with Lead Instructor approval)
- Guest sessions and masterclasses
- Curriculum feedback and suggestions

**Not Allowed:**
- Final curriculum approval
- Hiring/evaluating other trainers
- External representation without approval

### Availability Expectation
- Part-time commitment: 8-15 hours per month minimum
- Must commit to course cohort timelines once assigned
- 1 week notice for ad-hoc sessions

### Website Persona Elements
```
Photo: Professional headshot
Name: Full name
Title: "Subject Matter Expert — [Domain]"
Tagline: "Expert in [Domain] with [X]+ years experience"
Bio: 4-5 sentences covering background, expertise areas, and notable achievements
Domains: Primary and secondary teaching domains
Credentials: Relevant certifications, publications, talks
Notable Work: 2-3 highlighted projects or achievements
```

### Compensation Band
- Monthly retainer OR per-course basis
- Higher per-session rate than Tier 1
- Refer to compensation policy document

## Tier 3: Lead Instructor

### Profile
Senior experts who own entire courses end-to-end. They are responsible for course quality, curriculum relevance, and student outcomes. They mentor other trainers and represent Squarerootz externally.

### Minimum Requirements

| Criteria | Requirement | How to Verify |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Experience | 7+ years with progressive responsibility | Resume, LinkedIn, reference check |
| Leadership | Has built or led teams in their domain | Reference check, detailed discussion |
| Teaching mastery | Extensive training experience with proven outcomes | Past feedback, student testimonials, demo session |
| Curriculum design | Has designed courses or training programs before | Portfolio of past curricula, detailed discussion |
| Communication | Exceptional ability to simplify complex topics | Multiple evaluation rounds, demo session |
| Availability | Can commit regular hours weekly | Explicit agreement |
| Philosophy alignment | Believes in concepts-over-tools, production-first approach | Discussion, past content review |

### Preferred (Not Required)

- Industry recognition (awards, notable publications)
- Large professional following (LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube)
- Author of books or comprehensive technical content
- Startup founder or senior leadership experience
- Existing personal brand in AI/ML education

### Teaching Scope

**Full Authority:**
- Course ownership — accountable for entire course quality
- Curriculum design and updates
- Assessment and grading standards
- Student escalation handling
- Mentoring Tier 1 and Tier 2 trainers on their course

**With Approval:**
- External representation (conferences, podcasts, partnerships)
- Hiring recommendations for trainers in their domain
- Strategic input on new course development

### Availability Expectation
- Regular commitment: 15-25 hours per month minimum
- Must be available for cohort duration
- Responsive to escalations within 24-48 hours
- Quarterly planning participation

### Website Persona Elements
```
Photo: Professional headshot (high quality)
Name: Full name
Title: "Lead Instructor — [Course Name]"
Video: 60-90 second introduction video
Tagline: Personalized quote about teaching philosophy
Bio: Detailed 6-8 sentence bio covering career journey, expertise, achievements
Domains: All teaching domains
Credentials: Full list of certifications, publications, awards
Career History: Key roles and companies
Teaching Philosophy: 2-3 sentences on their approach
Notable Achievements: 3-5 highlighted accomplishments
Social Links: LinkedIn, GitHub, Twitter, personal website
```

### Compensation Band
- Monthly retainer (highest tier)
- Revenue share on owned courses (negotiable)
- Refer to compensation policy document

## Classification Process

### Step 1: Application / Nomination
- Trainer submits profile OR team nominates from pool
- Collect: Resume, LinkedIn, portfolio links, teaching samples (if any)

### Step 2: Initial Screening
**Conducted by:** Operations team
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Evaluate:**
- Basic eligibility for target tier
- Communication skills (first impression)
- Availability and interest level
- Domain alignment with current needs

**Outcome:** Proceed to technical evaluation OR reject with feedback

### Step 3: Technical Evaluation
**Conducted by:** Existing Lead Instructor or SME in relevant domain
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**For Tier 1:**
- Technical Q&A (15-20 min)
- Discussion of recent production work
- Scenario-based questions

**For Tier 2:**
- Technical deep dive (30 min)
- Curriculum understanding discussion
- Past teaching experience review

**For Tier 3:**
- Comprehensive technical interview (45 min)
- Curriculum design exercise
- Leadership and mentorship scenarios

**Outcome:** Tier recommendation OR reject with feedback

### Step 4: Demo Session
**Required for:** Tier 2 and Tier 3 (optional for Tier 1)
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Format:** Trainer teaches a topic of their choice

**Evaluation Criteria:**

| Criteria | Weight | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|------------------|
| Clarity | 25% | Explains concepts simply, logical flow |
| Engagement | 20% | Keeps attention, asks questions, interactive |
| Technical accuracy | 25% | Correct information, appropriate depth |
| Practical focus | 15% | Real-world examples, production relevance |
| Pace and timing | 15% | Appropriate speed, manages time well |

**Scoring:** 1-5 scale for each criterion
**Minimum to pass:** 3.5 average for Tier 2, 4.0 average for Tier 3

**Outcome:** Final tier assignment OR reject with feedback

### Step 5: Reference Check
**Required for:** Tier 2 and Tier 3
**Collect:** 2 professional references (past employers, colleagues, or students)

**Questions to ask:**
1. How do you know this person and for how long?
2. Can you describe their technical expertise?
3. Have you seen them teach or explain concepts? How effective were they?
4. How reliable are they with commitments?
5. Would you recommend them as a trainer? Why or why not?

### Step 6: Final Decision
**Decision maker:**
- Tier 1: Operations lead
- Tier 2: Lead Instructor + Operations lead
- Tier 3: Founder + Lead Instructor

**Document:** Classification decision, tier assignment, and any conditions

## Tier Movement

### Promotion (Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3)

**Eligibility:**
- Minimum 6 months at current tier
- Consistently positive session feedback (4.0+ average)
- No reliability or quality issues
- Expressed interest in higher tier
- Meets requirements of target tier

**Process:**
1. Self-nomination or manager nomination
2. Review of performance at current tier
3. Gap assessment against target tier requirements
4. Demo session / interview if needed
5. Decision by appropriate authority

### Demotion (Tier 3 → Tier 2 → Tier 1)

**Triggers:**
- Consistent negative feedback (below 3.5 average for 3+ sessions)
- Reliability issues (missed sessions, late cancellations)
- Technical inaccuracies reported by students
- Reduced availability below tier requirements
- Violation of trainer code of conduct

**Process:**
1. Documented feedback and warning
2. Improvement period (30-60 days)
3. Review of improvement
4. Demotion decision if no improvement
5. Option to exit if demotion not accepted

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can someone be classified in multiple tiers for different domains?**
A: Yes. A trainer might be Tier 3 in NLP but Tier 1 in Computer Vision. Classify per domain.

**Q: What if someone doesn't fit neatly into a tier?**
A: Use judgment. If they exceed some criteria but miss others, document the gaps and decide if they're acceptable. Consider conditional classification with review period.

**Q: How often should we re-evaluate classifications?**
A: Annually, or when a trainer requests promotion, or when performance issues arise.

**Q: Can external trainers (contractors) be Tier 3?**
A: Yes, but ensure contractual commitment matches Tier 3 expectations. Prefer longer-term contracts for Tier 3.

**Q: What if a great practitioner is a poor communicator?**
A: They may still be valuable for curriculum review or capstone evaluation, but not for live teaching. Consider an "Advisor" role outside the trainer tiers.
